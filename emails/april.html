Is your child having difficulty with reading comprehension? Here are some things you can do to help them! 
 
1. Have a discussion with your child about what they have read. Ask thought provoking questions such as "Why do you think this character made this choice?"
2. Make your child write down their thoughts as they read.
3. Read with your child in short segments and discuss after every paragraph or two to make sure they understand. 
4. Ask your child to make connections to their own life, a movie or another book. 
Tips for Tutors!
 
1. When correcting your student, first point out a strength and then point out the weakness. This helps build trust. 
2. Be clear when providing directions for your student so they can easily understand. 
3. Identify what students don't understand and then clarify. 
4. Ask students questions to help them paint a mental picture of the scene in the story. 
5. Let them make predictions. Ask questions like "What do you think will happen next? How can you tell?"
6. Model your reading strategy for half of the story and then let them do it on their own for the other half. Correct them as needed. 
Educational Game of the Month!

Learning a new culture. 

We all want to travel but can't right now. What if we could learn about a new culture from our home?

Pull up a map of the world (paper or online) or a globe if you have one. Have your child close their eyes and point to any place they want. Pick the country closest to where they point and learn about that culture! 

Research the following:
1. Country's history (how it came to be)
2. Country's history with the United States (relations)
3. Famous places to visit
4. Language and food

Then, pick a traditional dish and try to cook it for dinner together. Your children will be engaged and learn about a new culture!
